# Synthesis, Not Capture

The central insight across modern personal knowledge management literature is
that **capture is easy and synthesis is hard**. Every PKM tool that only
optimizes for capture (read-it-later apps, web clippers, auto-transcribers)
ends up creating a graveyard of content the user never revisits.

## Evidence from sources

- **Karpathy's LLM Wiki** (demo0001) argues the synthesis layer should be
  owned by an LLM, not by the human, precisely because synthesis is where the
  human gives up first.
- **Zettelkasten** (demo0002) has enforced this for a century through the
  rule of "atomic notes" — you cannot file a note until you have synthesized
  it into a single, self-contained idea.

## The synthesis gap

Capture produces **material**. Synthesis produces **knowledge**. The gap
between them is where most knowledge-worker productivity is lost. A person
with 10,000 captured articles and zero synthesis is worse off than a person
with 100 articles and solid synthesis, because the former has the illusion of
knowledge without the substance.

## Implication for tool design

A good PKM tool should be judged not by how much it helps you capture, but
by how much it helps you synthesize — or better, **how much it synthesizes
for you**.
